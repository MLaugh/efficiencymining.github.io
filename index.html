About the Invention

The Passive Electromagnetic Energy Harvester, invented by Tara Laughlin, is a novel hardware solution for recovering wasted energy from rotational systems. It is designed to be easily integrated into existing electromechanical infrastructure—without reliance on upstream software or complex redesign.

Technical Summary

The system uses magnetic induction: a rotor equipped with magnets spins near a stator coil, inducing electrical current from motion. Output can be regulated and optionally buffered via capacitors or auxiliary batteries. Compatible with a wide range of host platforms including mobility systems, industrial automation, and HVAC components.
